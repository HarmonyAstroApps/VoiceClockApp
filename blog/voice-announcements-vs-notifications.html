Voice Announcements vs Push Notifications

Short answer: Voice announcements deliver information passively (you hear it). Push notifications demand action (look, tap, dismiss). For time awareness, voice wins.

The Problem with Notifications

Push notifications break focus. You see the badge, feel the buzz, and your brain switches context. Even if you ignore it, the damage is done — you lost your flow.

Why Voice Announcements Win

  • Passive: You hear the time without action required
  • No badge: No visual clutter on your screen
  • Gentle: Announces one fact ("it's 2 PM") not "18 new messages"
  • No app switching: You stay in your work, not jumping to unlock your phone

The Research

Studies show notifications reduce focus by 23 minutes average. Voice announcements don't trigger the same attention-grabbing response. You stay productive.

Which Should You Choose?

For time awareness: voice announcements. For urgent messages: notifications. Voice Clock chose voice because time management shouldn't interrupt your work.
